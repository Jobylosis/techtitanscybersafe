ABOUT THE ADVOCACY

Youth Safety in Cyberspace aims to give awareness about the risks that the youth may encounter online, by providing guidance, education, and resources that will help them navigate and be protected in the digital world.

Young people aged 15 to 24 are more likely to use the Internet than the rest of the population. According to estimates, 82% of people aged between 15 and 24 used the Internet in 2025.

🌐

Cyberspace

The virtual space where people communicate and engage through interconnected networks. The internet has become essential for learning, communication, and entertainment.

Protection

ONLINE DANGERS

Threats that children and teens face in cyberspace

⚠️

Cyberbullying

Over a third of young people report being cyberbullied, with 1 in 5 skipping school because of it.

👁️

Online Exploitation

80% of children in 25 countries report feeling in danger of sexual abuse or exploitation online.

🔒

Privacy & Identity Theft

Sharing personal details can make young users vulnerable to identity theft, scams, and online predators.

⏰

Excessive Screen Time

Too much screen time negatively affects physical health, academic performance, sleep patterns, and mental wellbeing.

SAFETY TIPS

5 things every young person should know

01

Keep Personal Info Private

Never share your full name, address, school, phone number, or passwords online. Think twice before posting.

02

Be Careful Who You Talk To

Only interact with people you know and trust. Avoid meeting strangers without informing a parent or guardian.

03

Use Strong Passwords

Create strong, unique passwords. Use two-factor authentication and set social media profiles to private.

04

Report & Block Harmful Content

Report cyberbullying, scams, or inappropriate content immediately. Block users spreading negativity.

05

Balance Online & Offline

Limit screen time. Engage in hobbies, sports, and face-to-face interactions for mental and emotional health.
